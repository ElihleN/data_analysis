# Sales and Profit Analysis

This project analyses sales and profit performance using Python notebooks. It is designed to show a practical data analysis workflow: loading business data, cleaning and preparing it, exploring performance patterns, visualising results, and communicating business-relevant insights.

The project is suitable for portfolio review because it connects technical analysis with business interpretation. It does not only show code; it shows how data can be used to understand revenue, profitability, and performance drivers.

---

## Project Objectives

The main objectives of this project are to:

- Explore sales and profit patterns in a business dataset
- Identify high-performing and low-performing areas
- Compare sales and profitability across useful business dimensions
- Create clear visualisations for business interpretation
- Build a structured analysis workflow in Jupyter notebooks
- Communicate findings in a way that is understandable to technical and non-technical readers

---

## Business Questions

This project can help answer questions such as:

- What are the overall sales and profit trends?
- Which categories, products, regions, or customer groups contribute most to revenue?
- Are there areas with high sales but low or negative profit?
- Which parts of the business may require further investigation?
- How do sales and profit change across different groups or time periods?
- What insights can support better business decisions?

---

## Suggested Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Total Sales | Overall revenue generated from transactions. |
| Total Profit | Total profit after costs, losses, or discounts represented in the data. |
| Profit Margin | Profit divided by sales, useful for comparing profitability between groups. |
| Average Sales | Average revenue per transaction, product, or customer group. |
| Average Profit | Average profit per transaction, product, or customer group. |
| Loss-Making Groups | Products, categories, or segments where total profit is negative. |
